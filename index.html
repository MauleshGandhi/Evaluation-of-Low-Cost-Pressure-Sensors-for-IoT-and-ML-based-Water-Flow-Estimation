Evaluation of Low-Cost Pressure Sensors for IoT and ML-based Water Flow Estimation

INDICON 2025 Submitted
M. Gandhi • T. Bhatt • S. Chaudhari • A. Vattem
International Institute of Information Technology Hyderabad, India

Abstract

Water scarcity and inefficient resource management present critical challenges, with conventional flow measurement techniques often relying on expensive and invasive methods. This work evaluates the performance of three low-cost pressure sensors (HK3022, MBS3000, and Series 21Y) for internet of things (IoT) and machine learning (ML) based water flow estimation. Tests were conducted in a closed-loop experimental setup across pressure ranges from 1.0 to 2.0 bar, generating over 8,640 pressure readings and 2,160 flow measurements. Sensor performance was assessed through pressure measurement accuracy metrics (RMSE and CV), and flow estimation reliability was assessed using five ML algorithms. Results demonstrate that all sensors achieve comparable flow estimation accuracy. While calibration improves absolute pressure measurement accuracy, it provides minimal advantage for flow estimation tasks. This low-cost, non-invasive approach offers a promising water monitoring solution that is accessible and economically viable.

Keywords: flow estimation, IoT, ML, pressure sensors, sensor calibration

The Challenge

✓ Water Scarcity Crisis

Water scarcity and inefficient resource management present critical challenges globally. Conventional flow measurement techniques often rely on expensive and invasive methods.

✓ Expensive Traditional Methods

Traditional flow meters are expensive and invasive, making them challenging to deploy at scale, especially in resource-constrained regions.

✓ Limited Sensor Comparisons

Previous work validated the approach but was limited to a single sensor type and binary pressure states, lacking evaluation across different sensors and continuous pressure ranges.

✓ Need for Systematic Evaluation

A comprehensive comparison of low-cost pressure sensors is needed to determine their effectiveness for ML-based water flow estimation across different price points and operating conditions.

Machine Learning Approaches

Five ML algorithms were evaluated for both sensor calibration and flow rate estimation:

Linear Regression (LR)

Baseline model for linear relationships

Support Vector Regression (SVR)

Captures non-linear patterns

Random Forest (RF)

Ensemble decision tree method

Gradient Boosting (GB)

Advanced ensemble technique

XGBoost (XGB)

Optimized gradient boosting

Training: 80/20 train-test split with k-fold cross-validation. Models evaluated using RMSE and R² metrics. Two-stage analysis: (1) ML-based sensor calibration for Unit 1 sensors, (2) ML-based flow estimation for all sensors (calibrated and uncalibrated).

Key Findings

✓ All Low-Cost Sensors Perform Comparably

Despite 2.7x price difference, all sensors achieved similar flow estimation accuracy (R² > 0.997), making budget sensors viable alternatives.

✓ Calibration: Dual Benefits

Calibration dramatically improves pressure measurement (85-95% error reduction) but provides minimal advantage for flow estimation tasks.

✓ Position Independence

Sensor placement has minimal impact on estimation accuracy, allowing flexible deployment throughout water distribution systems.

✓ Simple Models Sufficient

Linear Regression achieved R² > 0.99, making it ideal for resource-constrained edge deployments despite ensemble methods performing slightly better.

✓ Strong Inverse Correlation

All sensors showed -0.99+ correlation between pressure and flow rate, validating the fundamental approach across different hardware.

Impact & Applications

  • Low-Cost Sensors Enable Widespread Deployment: Budget-friendly sensors (INR 1,500) perform comparably to expensive alternatives, enabling economically feasible large-scale monitoring.
  • Non-Invasive Installation: Pressure sensors can be installed without disrupting existing infrastructure or requiring pipeline modifications.
  • No Calibration Needed for Flow Estimation: Uncalibrated sensors with ML algorithms achieve excellent accuracy, simplifying deployment and maintenance.
  • Dual-Purpose Monitoring: With calibration, systems provide both accurate pressure readings (for leak detection, compliance) and flow estimates from single sensor deployment.
  • Flexible Positioning: Sensors can be deployed at various points in water distribution networks without compromising accuracy.
  • Computationally Efficient: Simple Linear Regression models work well, enabling edge deployment on resource-constrained IoT devices.
  • Scalable Solution: From residential to smart city applications, the approach scales economically across diverse infrastructure environments.
